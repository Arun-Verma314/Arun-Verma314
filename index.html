About Me

I am an M.Sc. Mathematics student at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kanpur. My mathematical journey is driven by a deep curiosity for abstract algebraic structures and a passion for finding intuitive ways to visualize and interpret them. This intersection of rigorous abstraction and geometric intuition is precisely why I am drawn to Topology, Algebra, and their synthesis in Algebraic Topology.

Currently, I am a Summer Research Project fellow at IISER Mohali, working in the fascinating field of Knot Theory. This project has been a major turning point in my mathematical growth. While my foundational background was primarily in point-set topology, delving into knot theory has completely expanded my perspective on mathematical abstraction. It has allowed me to experience the powerful applications of modern algebra—such as Combinatorial Group Theory and Geometric Group Theory—in solving topological problems.

I am deeply fascinated by how we can use tools like the fundamental group, braids, and the Artin braid group to study topological spaces.
